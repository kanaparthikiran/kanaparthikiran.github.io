## What I do

I'm a Principal Engineer focused on the boundary between distributed systems
and AI — specifically, building agentic AI platforms that operate inside
enterprise environments without breaking the trust contract those
environments depend on.

Most recently that has meant production multi-agent pipelines (LangGraph),
Model Context Protocol (MCP) tool frameworks, retrieval pipelines, and the
evaluation/telemetry harness that keeps agent quality measurable as the
underlying models and prompts evolve.

Before AI took over my mornings, I spent twenty years building large-scale
backend platforms, distributed messaging, multi-language build infrastructure
(Bazel at scale), and developer-acceleration tooling that hundreds of
engineers depend on.

## How I work

- **Architecture by construction.** I trust diagrams less than running code.
  Reference implementations, SDKs, and IaC over slide decks.
- **Bounded authority for AI.** When an LLM has write permissions on a real
  system, defense-in-depth is non-negotiable: classification gates, tool
  allowlists, sandbox-isolated runtimes, deterministic provenance, full audit
  trails. Every layer is independent.
- **Evaluation as a flywheel.** Agent quality is a property you measure, not
  a property you hope for. Baselines and rolling-history telemetry are the
  unglamorous work that keeps production agents honest.
- **Small, correct primitives.** A planner that emits one JSON object beats
  one that emits prose. A tool with a typed schema beats five tools with
  loose contracts.
